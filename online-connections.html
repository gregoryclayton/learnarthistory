Online Collections

High-quality digital collections you can access from anywhere. Many provide open licenses for study and teaching; always check usage terms.

Global Aggregators

  • Google Arts & Culture: Aggregated high-res images and virtual museum tours; good for quick comparisons.
  • Europeana: European cultural heritage portal with millions of items and filters by rights.
  • Digital Public Library of America (DPLA): U.S. libraries, archives, and museums aggregated with metadata and rights info.

Open Access Highlights

  • Rijksmuseum (Netherlands): Extensive open-access images; download high-res files.
  • Smithsonian Open Access (U.S.): Millions of items with open licenses.
  • The Met Open Access (U.S.): Large set of public-domain images with zoomable views.
  • Cleveland Museum of Art: Open-access images and IIIF support for deep zoom.
  • National Gallery of Art (Washington, D.C.): Open images and metadata; strong European and American holdings.

Specialized Archives

  • MoMA Online Collection: Modern and contemporary focus; exhibition histories.
  • Getty Search Gateway: Collections and scholarly resources; includes high-res photography.
  • British Museum Collection Online: Strong antiquities, prints, and drawings; rights vary by item.
  • Library of Congress Digital Collections: Prints, photographs, maps; many public-domain.
  • Wellcome Collection: Medical history, science, and art intersections; many open-licensed images.

Viewing & Research Tools

  • IIIF Viewers (e.g., Mirador): Deep-zoom tools; many museums provide IIIF manifests for side-by-side comparison.
  • Reverse Image Search: Helpful for tracing provenance of widely shared images.
  • Metadata Filters: Use rights/license filters (CC0, Public Domain) to find reusable images.

Tip

When studying a work, download or open the highest-resolution file available to inspect brushwork, craquelure, or tool marks. Pair with chapter readings for context.
